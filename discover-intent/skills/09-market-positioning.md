# 09 — Market Positioning

**Goal:** Honest competitive assessment including indirect competitors, with a positioning map that reflects reality rather than wishful thinking.

**When to use independently:** Before any go-to-market work, or when a team believes they have "no real competitors."

---

## Question Matrix

| # | Question | What It Exposes | Evidence Type |
|---|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| 1 | Who are all of your competitors? Include direct competitors, indirect competitors, and the option of doing nothing. Spreadsheets, agencies, manual processes, and "we just don't do this" all count. | Complete competitive landscape vs the sanitized version that only includes direct competitors | Stated |
| 2 | Why has a well-funded, talented team not solved this already? If one has — what is actually different about your approach, not your messaging? | Market awareness and honest differentiation assessment | Stated |
| 3 | What do you know that the market does not? Is this a genuine information edge, a technical insight, or a belief you hope is true? | Real edge vs rationalized differentiation | Stated |
| 4 | Is your differentiation defensible? What specifically would stop a competitor from copying the key part in three months? | Moat reality — sustainable vs temporary advantage | Stated |
| 5 | If a well-funded competitor decided to kill you specifically, what would they do in the first six months? Do you have an answer to that move? | Threat modeling and strategic resilience | Stated |
| 6 | What is your user's next-best alternative if you do not exist? How painful is it? How often do they choose it? | True competitive bar — the experience you must meaningfully beat | Behavioral |
| 7 | Where on the market are you positioned today versus where you want to be in 12 months? What closes that gap? | Positioning gap and whether the path is realistic | Stated |
| 8 | What does your ideal customer already believe is true about this category? What do they believe is true about you? | Positioning fit and the messaging distance you need to travel | Inferred |
| 9 | Which customer segment do you win first — the beachhead — before expanding? Why do you win there and nowhere else yet? | Beachhead strategy and focus discipline | Stated |
| 10 | What do your competitors do better than you right now? Be specific and honest — not "they have more resources." | Honest capability gap that affects positioning | Stated |
| 11 | What would a customer say about you after switching from a competitor? What specific phrases would they use? | Positioning validation through user language | Behavioral |
| 12 | In 18 months, what market conditions would make your current positioning irrelevant? How would you know early? | Positioning durability and early warning signals | Inferred |

## Follow-up Probes (use when confidence < 70%)

- You said competitors have not solved this because [reason]. What evidence do you have for that? Have you talked to users of those competitors?
- Your differentiation is [X]. A competitor can replicate that in [estimated time]. What do you have after [X] is copied?
- You named the beachhead as [segment]. What is the win rate you expect in that segment, and what experiments have you run to test it?
- The indirect competitor "doing nothing" — what would it take for a user to choose that over you? How common is that outcome?
- You said users would describe switching to you as [words]. Have you heard those words from a user, or are those the words you hope to hear?

---

## Contradiction Patterns to Watch

| Pattern | Signal |
|---------|--------|
| No competitor syndrome | "We have no real competitors" — nearly always false |
| Feature moat | Differentiation is a single feature with no network effect, switching cost, or data advantage |
| Wishful positioning | Positioned against weak competitors instead of the real substitute behavior |
| Beachhead avoidance | Refuses to narrow to a specific first segment; wants to win "everyone" first |
| Copied language | Positioning statement uses words competitors also use without distinction |

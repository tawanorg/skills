# 01 — Motivation Audit

**Goal:** Expose whether this project should exist, why you are the one building it, and what emotional forces are actually driving it.

**When to use independently:** Before committing time or money to any idea. Run this first, always.

---

## Question Matrix

| # | Question | What It Exposes | Evidence Type |
|---|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| 1 | Why are **you** building this and not someone else? What is your actual unfair advantage — not the one that sounds good in a pitch? | Real positioning vs borrowed confidence | Stated |
| 2 | What is the emotional reason behind this project you have not said out loud yet? Not the professional version — the raw one. | Hidden driver that will shape every scope decision | Stated |
| 3 | Are you building what users need or what you would enjoy building? How do you actually know the difference in this case? | Builder bias vs user-centered thinking | Requires validation |
| 4 | Map every stakeholder: who benefits, who loses power or money, who has veto authority, who is the real economic buyer, who signs off. | Power dynamics and hidden blockers | Stated + verifiable |
| 5 | If this project disappeared tomorrow with no explanation, who would notice first? What would they do instead? How long until operations resumed normally? | True demand signal vs convenience demand | Behavioral |
| 6 | Who told you to build this? What is their incentive — stated and unstated? | Proxy motivation, political pressure | Stated |
| 7 | Have you tried to talk yourself out of this idea? What was the strongest argument against it, and what survived? | Intellectual honesty, conviction quality | Stated |
| 8 | What would you do if someone showed you credible proof this will not work? Be specific — what action would you take tomorrow? | Commitment type: learning vs ego protection | Stated |
| 9 | Which version of success would make you proudest in five years? Now which version are you actually building toward? Are those the same? | Alignment between stated and revealed goals | Stated |
| 10 | What does "done" look like for you personally? Revenue? Impact? Exit? Recognition? Resume line? | Exit criteria and success definition | Stated |
| 11 | Who loses if this succeeds? Name them specifically. Have you thought about their response? | Competitive and organizational threat awareness | Stated |
| 12 | If you strip away every feature and ship only the single thing that proves the core idea — what is it? Can you live with that version? | Minimum commitment threshold | Stated |

---

## Follow-up Probes (use when confidence < 70%)

- You said [X is the reason] — but when you described the project earlier you emphasized [Y]. Which is actually driving decisions?
- You framed this as a user problem, but everything you described maps to your own frustration. What user behavior have you directly observed that is separate from your experience?
- What evidence do you have that this need is real vs imagined by someone like you?
- When did you last talk to someone who is not already sympathetic to this idea?
- What is the version of this project you are embarrassed to admit you want to build?

---

## Contradiction Patterns to Watch

| Pattern | Signal |
|---------|--------|
| Stated motivation vs revealed motivation | Says "user need" but describes personal frustration throughout |
| Stakeholder blind spot | Identifies only beneficiaries, cannot name who loses |
| Commitment mismatch | "I'll pivot if data says so" but cannot articulate what data would trigger a pivot |
| Ego protection | Responds defensively when asked to describe the failure case |
| Borrowed conviction | "Everyone says this is a good idea" without naming who or why |
